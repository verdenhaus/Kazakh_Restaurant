About Us

Discover our restaurant story

In the heart of the Kazakh steppe, where endless skies meet boundless hospitality, a rich culinary tradition was born. For centuries, nomads journeyed across these vast lands, creating dishes that celebrated the bounty of the earth and the warmth of community. Our restaurant brings this heritage to life with tender beshbarmak, smoky shashlyk, golden baursaks, and the soothing comfort of Kazakh tea. Each dish is a story, crafted to honor the traditions of the steppe and the spirit of togetherness that defines Kazakh culture. Welcome to our table, where every meal is an invitation to savor history and warmth.

Baursak

Baursak is a delicious puffy fried bread served on special occasions all over Kazakhstan. This isn’t an everyday bread, but something for an important party, like a wedding, or even a memorial. It is said that the smell of the oil and the frying baursak floats high into the sky so that your dead loved ones can feed on the aroma and enjoy them with you. It’s a beautiful thought as you fry.

Whether served with tea, paired with savory dishes, or shared during festive gatherings, baursak embodies the warmth and generosity of Kazakh culture. Simple yet deeply meaningful, this beloved treat is more than food—it’s a connection to heritage and the joy of sharing.

Beshbarmak

Beshbarmak, meaning "five fingers," is the heart of Kazakh cuisine and a dish steeped in tradition. This hearty meal features tender, boiled meat—often lamb, beef, or horse—served over wide noodles and topped with a rich onion broth. Traditionally eaten by hand, beshbarmak symbolizes community and togetherness, often shared during celebrations or family gatherings.

Its comforting flavors and cultural significance make it more than just a dish; it's a taste of Kazakh heritage and a celebration of the bonds that unite us.

Frequently asked questions

What is the specialty of your restaurant?

We specialize in authentic Kazakh cuisine, including signature dishes like beshbarmak, kazy, baursak, and traditional beverages like kumys and m shubat.

Do you offer vegetarian or vegan options?

Currently, we do not offer vegetarian options, as our menu focuses on traditional Kazakh meat-based dishes.

Can I book a table for a large group or host an event?

Absolutely! We welcome group reservations and offer a cozy space for celebrations, corporate events, or family gatherings. Contact us for bookings and special arrangements.

Are children welcome at the restaurant?

Yes, we are family-friendly and offer a welcoming environment for guests of all ages.

What traditional drinks are available at the restaurant?

We serve kumys (fermented mare’s milk), shubat (fermented camel’s milk), and traditional teas with milk or herbs, giving you an authentic taste of Kazakh hospitality.

Do you have live music or cultural performances?

Yes, we often feature traditional Kazakh music performances and cultural events to enhance your dining experience. Check our schedule for upcoming events!
